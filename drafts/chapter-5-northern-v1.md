Chapter 5: Gilgit-Baltistan & Azad Kashmir – Mountain Treasures
The northern mountainous regions produce wholesome, warming foods using local apricots, walnuts, buckwheat, and dairy to combat cold climates.
Famous dishes: Chapshuro (savory meat-filled pastry similar to a pie), Harissa (slow-cooked wheat and meat porridge for winter), Mumtu (steamed dumplings), walnut cake, dried apricot soups, buckwheat breads, and butter tea.
History and Origin: Blend of Central Asian, Tibetan, and ancient Silk Road influences; Hunza valley foods linked to longevity traditions using pure glacier water and organic ingredients.
Cultural Importance: Meals sustain high-altitude life—shared during long winters, polo matches, and harvest festivals; symbolize endurance and community warmth.
